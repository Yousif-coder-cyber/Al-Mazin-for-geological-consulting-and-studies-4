Al-Mazin Bureau for Geological Consulting and Studies

About Us

Al-Mazin Bureau for Geological Studies and Consulting was established in (1998) and includes a distinguished group of consultants in various fields of geology, many of whom have worked for years in relevant government departments and institutions. The Bureau’s future vision goes beyond the traditional role of consulting firms, aiming to develop ambitious strategic plans to position Iraq in the place it rightfully deserves.

Our Achievments

  • Preparing an environmental impact assessment study for the borders of Basra Governorate.
  • Preparing an environmental impact assessment study for the establishment of Sinjar University.
  • Studying and calculating the amount of pollution in the Tigris River in the Al-Qaim area.
  • Studying and evaluating environmental and radiological pollution in the Abu Sukheir area, Najaf Al-Ashraf.
  • Drilling water wells for various purposes such as agricultural, irrigation, and reducing groundwater levels, in addition to drilling monitoring wells.
  • Studying surface and groundwater resources and the study of hydraulic factors, in addition to studying mineral water sources and the possibility of establishing health resorts.
  • Exploration and investigation of salt deposits and determining their reserves.
  • Various geophysical surveys for the purposes of exploration, identifying cavities, and determining buried objects.
  • Studying and determining phosphate reserves in the Al-Ukhaidhir area.
  • Geotechnical assessment of the Oshwa and Jandali sites for sanitary landfill purposes.
  • Geotechnical assessment of the site between the Khazka and Barkhiya valleys, west of the city of Ana, for the purpose of constructing an artificial lake for pumped-storage hydroelectricity generation.
  • Geotechnical assessment of the Shari Juan site in the Sulaymaniyah Governorate for the construction of the Grand Millennium Hotel (32 floors).
  • Geotechnical assessment of previous presidential sites such as Makhoul and Sarsang.
  • Preparing an engineering geological map of the area between Baiji and Sinjar.
  • Geotechnical investigation of marble and its alternatives throughout the country.
  • Updating hydrogeological maps at a scale of 1:250,000.
  • Preparing a map of groundwater depth in Iraq at a scale of 1:6,000,000.
  • Implementing grouting operations using micropiles under foundations to treat land subsidence and subsurface cavities.
